Present Perfect in daily life

Use it when the result affects now, or for experiences with no specific time.

Structure

Subject + have/has + Past Participle (V3)

Time words: already, just, yet, ever, never, so far, recently, lately.

Main uses
  • Result connected to now — I have finished the report.
  • Experience — She has never flown.
  • Recent action — He has just called.
Classroom/work examples
  • I have uploaded the attendance file.
  • Students have submitted their portfolios.
  • We have revised the lesson plan.
Contrast with Past Simple
  • Present Perfect: I have eaten already. (focus on result)
  • Past Simple: I ate at 7 pm. (focus on finished time)
Common mistakes
  • Wrong: I have finished yesterday. don’t use specific past time
  • Right: I finished yesterday. / I have finished already.
Quick exercises
  1. I ______ (finish) marking. → I have finished marking.
  2. She ______ (not/submit) yet. → She hasn’t submitted yet.
  3. We ______ (just/update) the website. → We have just updated the website.
