Necessary tenses in daily life

Most-used tenses: Present Simple, Past Simple, Future Simple, Present Perfect.

Go to Necessary Tenses
Present simple tense

Facts, routines, general truths (I live in Satun. She works every day.).

Past simple tense

Completed actions in the past with a clear time (Yesterday, I watched a movie.).

Future simple tense

Decisions or plans made at the moment of speaking (I will call you later.).

Present perfect tense

Experiences/results connected to now (I have finished my homework.).

Daily-life examples
Possessive pronoun

mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs — replace a noun and show ownership.

Passive voice

be + past participle (V3); emphasize the result rather than the doer (The report was submitted.).
